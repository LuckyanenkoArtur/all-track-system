# What we neend to implement in Finance

1. A **Balance Sheet** is a financial statement that shows the company's financial position at a specific point in time.

> Assets = Liabilities + Owner's Equity
> 

> ***Purpose:** To determine the company's overall financial health.*

2. An **Income Statement** (also called a **Profit and Loss Statement**) summarizes revenues and expenses over a period of time. It shows:

- Revenue earned,
- Expenses incurred,
- Net income or net loss.

> **Net Income = Revenues − Expenses**
> 

> ***Purpose:** To measure profitability.*

2. An **Accounts Receivable Report** lists money owed to the business by customers who purchased on credit.

***It usually includes:***

- Customer names,
- Invoice numbers,
- Amounts due,
- Due dates,
- Outstanding balances.

***Purpose:** To monitor collections and identify overdue customer payments.*

### 4. Sales reports

A **Sales Report** summarizes the company's sales activities. It may show:

- Total sales,
- Sales by product,
- Sales by customer,
- Sales by salesperson,
- Sales trends over time.

> ***Purpose:** To evaluate revenue performance and identify best-selling products.*
> 

### 5. Purchasing reports

A **Purchasing Report** records purchases made from suppliers.

***It contains information such as:***

- Items purchased,
- Suppliers,
- Purchase dates,
- Quantities,
- Purchase costs.

> ***Purpose:** To analyze spending patterns and manage procurement activities.*
>

### 6. Accounts Payable Reports

*An **Accounts Payable Report** lists amounts the business owes to suppliers and creditors. It includes:*

- Vendor names,
- Bills due,
- Due dates,
- Outstanding balances.

> ***Purpose:** To ensure bills are paid on time and manage cash obligations.*


### 7. Account Registers

An **Account Register** is a detailed record of all transactions affecting a particular account.

Examples include:

- Cash register,
- Bank account register,
- Credit card register.

It shows:

- Running balances.
- Dates,
- Descriptions,
- Deposits,
- Withdrawals,

> ***Purpose:** To track the activity and balance of individual accounts.*
